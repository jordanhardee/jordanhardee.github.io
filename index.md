## Who, What, When, Where

## Why
Mason City Council is extremely conservative, and I think there are enough progressives in Mason to entail us to at least one seat on city council.

If you weren't aware, Mason City Council can be a bit of a circus. There is so much in-fighting, so much toxicity, so much partisan non-sense. The people of Mason deserve better.
I really believe if elected that I can help bring City Council together and help to make it the efficient organ of civil power it was supposed to be. There comes a point in time 
when you realize "if I don't do this, I'm not sure anyone else will". Watching a City Council meeting on IRCTV as I often do, I had this
realization. "Someone has to do something", so I decided to run for office.

I get it, I'm young. Relative to most people in government, I am very young. Heck, like most people my age, especially with the COVID pandemic, I live with my parents and we 
don't even agree on politics. But I don't see this as a weakness, rather I see it as an asset. 
I don't despise people I don't agree politically precisely because I come from a politically diverse family. As a Green I am critical of both parties. America is a gerentocracy, what it
needs more than anything else is young blood in the reams of government. If you don't even know how the modern world works you shouldn't be writing laws for it. It's our turn.

Being a legislator would be a paradigm shift for me. I am used to writing on politics, the most I do is organize locally. Being elected to public office, actually being in power, 
is so much more than saying what ought to be
done, it means doing it. 

I have compiled a document detailing my [views](views.pdf) on many of the issues that face us today and what I intend to do as a legislator. This is not comprehensive as there are so, so many issues that face us today. But this is a general summary of where I stand on a variety of issues.
If a section is lacking or is not satisfactory, please refer to the website of the [US Green Party](https://gp.org) as there's a good chance I agree with my party.
